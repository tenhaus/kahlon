about KAHLON:

challenges the boundaries of genre specific music events and present new wave artists with distinctive styles on one stage to a colorful influx of music enthusiast to cultivate a fresh take on traditional showcasing.

KAHLON is curated by Baltimore-based rapper Abdu Ali with assistance by True Laurels editor Lawrence Burney.

team KAHLON:


Curator, Abdu Ali

Rooted in the ghettos of Baltimore, Abdu Ali raps, sings, and chats over unorthodox sounds, creating music that’s unfamiliar, provoking, and #rare.

Recently he has been inspired to DJ focusing on mainly music genres of subcultures from around the world especially his hometown sound, Baltimore Club.

Abdu being from Baltimore and witnessing the rich music culture it had in the late 90s and early 2000s was inspired to bring that energy back and revitalize the music scene in Baltimore by putting on KAHLON.

Assistant Curator, Lawrence Burney

Lawrence Burney is a Baltimore based writer whose music zine, True Laurels , honors the unique stories of defiant artists through diaries, feature stories and photography which tend to spark insight on cultural matters outside of the artists’ work.

Burney’s music coverage has also been in publications such as Pitchfork, XXL, Noisey, Complex, Frank 151 and Baltimore’s City Paper.

Being raised around Baltimore’s colorful music scene, Lawrence is dedicated to helping bring the current one to its full potential by seeking out the East Coast’s most promising artists and presenting them to KAHLON’s audience

Space Visual Designer, Johnny Rogers

Baltimore native Johnny Rogers (JEDICOM) is the video assemblage artist behind Kahlon’s visuals.

Armed with 8 projectors, he creates immersive “video wall paper” for Kahlon utilizing vintage computers, analog video gear, and found material.

Johnny has worked in a variety of contexts ranging from museums, music venues, and theaters, to warehouses, shopping malls, and the internet.

He’s collaborated with artists such as Future Farmers, George Kuchar, and Martin Schmidt and has performed and shown work at the SFMOMA, Baltimore Contemporary Museum, Yerba Buena Center for Art, and Pacific Design Center. From 2007-2011 his projection craft was honed while playing “video mixer” for techno-metal band Abiku, performing 200+ shows and 7+ months touring.

Johnny makes playful and visually distinct installations, animated works, performances, music videos, and net art.

Promotions, Alejandra Isabel (a.k.a Ale)

Ale was born in Florida and has been living in Baltimore since 2011.

Collaborative work began with co-curating Process Collective’s first show and continued with video projects and performative group work over the following year.

Most recently, she has pursued show promotion and music under the DJ name Genie.

As a current member of BALTI GURLS, she is involved in supporting women of color and diverse backgrounds in the arts.

Ale is a dedicated member of KAHLON’s promotion team and hopes to help it expand even further.

Promotions, Chanel Cruz

Born in D.C., Chanel Cruz moved to Baltimore to pursue a Bachelor of Fine Arts at the Maryland Institute College of Art.

She is currently a Fibers major and Video/ Film concentration at the Institution.

By the beginning of college she had already interned for Smithsonian National Portrait Gallery, Corcoran Gallery of Art, and presented her first fashion show.

During her sophomore year she directed MICA’s 21st Annual Benefit Fashion Show and is currently a Stylist at Madewell.

Naturally so Chanel, gravitated to Baltimore’s underground music scene and met rapper Abdu Ali, with a yearning to support the movement of KAHLON.
